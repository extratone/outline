The most-shared tweets made by Russian trolls that Twitter scrubbed from the internet

They might surprise you.

Jon Christian Feb—14—2018 01:20PM EST

This morning, NBC News published a database of 200,000 tweets that it says Twitter has tied to a campaign by a Russia-linked propaganda group called the Internet Research Association. The tweets were intended to disrupt the United States’ presidential election, according to the report.

Twitter has scrubbed the tweets from its site, and most are not backed up by organizations that archive online content. Some were boilerplate political posts boosting Donald Trump and criticizing Hillary Clinton. But several appear to be impersonating Black Lives Matter activists, which was a way of blending in on Twitter to make their nefarious activity less noticeable. Links and images that were in the tweets are now removed, making the context of some unclear (this also means they can't be embedded or screenshotted, which is why some are in plain text).

Twitter had previously informed 1.4 million of its users that they had interacted with accounts associated with the Internet Research Agency. The list of tweets published by NBC News was assembled from a list of accounts provided to Congress by Twitter, according to the report.
